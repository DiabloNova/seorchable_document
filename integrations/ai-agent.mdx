The جستجوپذیر AI Agent is an automated analysis engine built directly into your dashboard. Once enabled, it runs in the background around the clock — scanning your AI visibility data, detecting meaningful shifts in your rankings and citations, and surfacing actionable insights without you needing to log in and check manually. Think of it as a vigilant analyst that never sleeps and never misses a change.

## Enable the AI Agent

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open AI Agent settings">
    In your جستجوپذیر dashboard, go to **Settings → AI Agent**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure monitoring frequency">
    Choose how often the agent scans your data:

    - **Real-time** — continuous scanning, alerts within minutes of a change
    - **Daily** — one full scan per day, ideal for stable campaigns
    - **Weekly** — summary scan each week, best for long-term trend tracking
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set alert thresholds">
    Define the sensitivity of your alerts. For example:

    - Alert when my ranking for a tracked keyword drops **more than 10%**
    - Alert when a competitor's citation rate rises **more than 15%** in a 7-day window
    - Alert when a new high-intent topic appears where I have **zero citations**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose notification channels">
    Select where the agent sends its alerts:

    - **Email** — delivered to any address on your account
    - **Slack** — connect your workspace via OAuth in **Settings → Integrations**
    - **Webhook** — POST the alert payload to any URL (see the payload format below)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the agent">
    Toggle the agent on and click **Save**. The agent begins its first scan immediately. You will receive a confirmation notification on your chosen channel once the initial scan is complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  When you first enable the AI Agent, set your thresholds lower than you think you need them. A week of alerts — even noisy ones — will quickly show you the normal variance range for your data, and you can raise the thresholds with confidence once you understand the baseline.
</Tip>

## Alert Types

The AI Agent generates three categories of alerts:

**Ranking change alerts** notify you when a tracked keyword's AI citation position moves beyond your configured threshold — either upward (good news) or downward (action required).

**New citation opportunity alerts** fire when the agent detects a high-intent topic or question that competitors are being cited for but your content does not yet address.

**Competitor move alerts** trigger when a monitored competitor gains a significant number of new AI citations or loses a substantial share of its current citations, signalling a strategic shift worth reacting to.

## Webhook Payload Format

When you configure a webhook notification channel, the AI Agent posts a JSON payload to your endpoint for every alert. Use this format to build integrations with Slack (custom), PagerDuty, Zapier, or any internal tooling:

```json
{
  "event": "ranking_change",
  "timestamp": "2024-11-15T09:42:00Z",
  "workspace_id": "ws_abc123",
  "alert": {
    "type": "ranking_drop",
    "severity": "high",
    "keyword": "project management software",
    "previous_rank": 2,
    "current_rank": 7,
    "change_pct": -28.5,
    "ai_engine": "ChatGPT",
    "threshold_pct": 10
  },
  "page": {
    "url": "https://example.com/blog/project-management",
    "ai_readiness_score": 64,
    "citation_rate_7d": 0.12
  },
  "recommended_action": "Review page content against the AI-readiness checklist and update structured data."
}
```

Respond to the webhook with HTTP `200` to acknowledge receipt. The agent retries failed deliveries up to three times with exponential back-off.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How is the AI Agent different from manual dashboard checks?">
    Manual dashboard checks show you a snapshot of the moment you log in. The AI Agent monitors continuously, retains historical context, and applies statistical analysis to determine whether a change is meaningful noise or a genuine trend — then it comes to you. You avoid the cognitive overhead of deciding what to look for, and you get notified only when something genuinely warrants attention.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I customize which metrics the agent monitors?">
    Yes. From **Settings → AI Agent → Monitored Metrics**, you can enable or disable monitoring for individual metrics including citation rate, share of AI voice, AI-readiness score, keyword-level ranking, and competitor citation counts. You can also scope monitoring to specific pages, keyword groups, or domains.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I connect the agent to Slack?">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations → Slack** and click **Connect Workspace**. Authorise جستجوپذیر in the Slack OAuth flow, then choose which channel should receive agent alerts. Once connected, return to **Settings → AI Agent** and select **Slack** as a notification channel.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
