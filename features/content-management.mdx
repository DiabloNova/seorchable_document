Getting cited by AI search engines isn't a matter of luck — it depends on whether your content is structured, comprehensive, and credible enough for models like ChatGPT and Perplexity to trust as a source. جستجوپذیر's Content Management feature gives you a centralized workspace to audit every page on your site, understand its current AI readiness, apply targeted improvements, and measure the citation gains those improvements produce.

## Content Audit

Before you can improve your content's AI visibility, you need to know where you stand. The content audit scans your entire connected site and assigns each page an **AI Readiness Score** — a composite measure of how well a page is positioned to earn citations from AI search engines.

The audit categorizes pages into three buckets:

- **AI-Ready** — Pages that meet best-practice thresholds for structure, keyword coverage, schema markup, and content depth. These pages are actively competing for AI citations.
- **Needs Improvement** — Pages that are partially optimized but have identifiable gaps. Targeted fixes here typically produce the fastest citation gains.
- **Not Optimized** — Pages with significant structural or content deficiencies. These represent the largest opportunity if you have the capacity to rebuild or substantially expand them.

Run a fresh audit at any time from the **Content Management** home screen. Audits refresh automatically on a weekly basis, or immediately after you publish updates to tracked pages.

## Optimization Recommendations

Every page in your content inventory comes with a per-page optimization checklist generated by جستجوپذیر's AI analysis engine. Recommendations are specific and actionable — not vague advice. Examples include:

- **Title improvement** — Your current title is 42 characters and omits the primary keyword. Suggested rewrite provided.
- **Structured data** — This page lacks `Article` or `FAQPage` schema. Adding schema markup increases the likelihood of being cited as a definitive source.
- **Keyword density** — The term "enterprise resource planning" appears only once in 1,200 words. Increase natural usage to signal topical focus.
- **Content depth** — Competitor pages ranking for your target prompts average 1,800 words. This page is 650 words and omits three subtopics AI models commonly cite.

Recommendations are sorted by estimated impact, so you can work through them in priority order without having to decide what matters most.

## Content Performance Tracking

Making changes is only half the job — you also need to confirm they worked. Content Management tracks the AI citation performance of every page over time, so you can directly measure the effect of each optimization you apply.

After you update a page and mark the change in جستجوپذیر, the platform begins tracking citation volume for that URL against its pre-update baseline. If citations increase, the improvement is credited to your change log. If they don't move, you'll see that clearly too — and the recommendations panel will update with new suggestions.

## How to Optimize a Page

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Content Management">
    Select **Content** from the main navigation in your جستجوپذیر dashboard. You'll see your full page inventory with AI Readiness Scores displayed beside each URL.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a Page from Your Inventory">
    Click any page to open its detail view. Pages marked **Needs Improvement** or **Not Optimized** are the best starting points. Sort the inventory by AI Readiness Score ascending to surface the weakest pages first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the AI Readiness Score">
    The detail view shows a score breakdown by category: structure, keyword coverage, content depth, schema markup, and authority signals. Each category has its own sub-score so you can immediately see which dimension is dragging the overall score down.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply Recommended Improvements">
    Work through the recommendations checklist from highest impact to lowest. Each item includes a brief explanation of why it matters and, where applicable, a suggested edit or template to make implementation faster. Check off items as you complete them in your CMS.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track Performance Changes">
    Return to the page detail view after publishing your changes. Toggle to the **Citation Trends** tab to watch citation volume shift over the following days. Use the before/after annotation tool to mark the date of your update for clean before-vs-after comparison.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  AI-ready content shares a few universal traits: it answers a specific question directly in the opening paragraph, uses clear heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3) to signal structure to language models, includes at least one structured data type (FAQ, HowTo, or Article schema), and links internally to related authoritative pages on your site. Hitting all four of these on a page that previously missed them routinely produces measurable citation gains within two to four weeks.
</Tip>

For element-level tuning of titles, meta descriptions, headings, and schema on individual pages, see [On-Page Optimization](/features/on-page-optimization). Content Management handles full-page strategy; On-Page Optimization drills into the specific HTML elements that AI models parse first.
